Hello, and welcome.

If you're reading this, you signed up at [awonderfullife.ca](https://awonderfullife.ca). Thank you. This is the very first issue of the newsletter, and I'll keep it simple: a hello, a short tour of what's new, and a promise about what comes next.

## Why a newsletter

For a while now, this has been a quiet corner where I write about *data, life, and the space between*: the things I keep turning over. Technology and how it reshapes ordinary days. Politics on both sides of the Canada–US border. The occasional travelogue. And the reflections that don't fit any neat box. The newsletter is just a way to bring the new pieces to you directly, instead of hoping you wander back.

## What's been happening

The site recently went through a full redesign: a calmer, more editorial look, an original illustration on every essay, and browsing by theme (Reflection, Society, Politics, Technology, and Travel). A few places to start, if you're new here:

- [Finding Stability in Unfinished Work](https://awonderfullife.ca/posts/finding-stability-in-unfinished-work-a-guide-to-endurance.html): on endurance, and making peace with the things that are never quite done.
- [Data Dilemma: Starving for Truth in a Sea of Misinformation](https://awonderfullife.ca/posts/data-dilemma-starving-for-truth-in-a-sea-of-misinformation.html): the essay closest to this blog's name.
- [How Apple's AI Enhances Everyday Life](https://awonderfullife.ca/posts/apple-intelligence-ai-for-us-everyday-users.html): technology where it actually meets the day.
- [Whispers of Wanderlust](https://awonderfullife.ca/posts/whispers-of-wanderlust-an-eco-friendly-journey-across-canada.html): a greener way to see Canada.

There are sixteen essays now, and you can [browse them all by theme](https://awonderfullife.ca/archive.html).

## What's ahead

The short version of the road ahead:

- A new essay every week, carried across those same five threads.
- A few small, genuinely useful tools on the site. Nothing that tracks you, just handy.
- And the same care in how it all reads and looks.

## The weekly promise

So here's the deal: one new post a week, with this newsletter so you never have to remember to check. No noise, no daily clutter, just the new piece, each Saturday evening.

Thank you for being here at the very start. Reply any time; it reaches me directly.

Yours,
